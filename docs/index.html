Operative Note Generator

Draft common general surgery operation notes from a short form. The page has no account, backend, analytics or autosave.

Open generator View source

What it is

A browser form for turning operation details into a draft note. Check it, edit it, then copy it into the clinical record yourself.

Why it exists

Operation notes are repetitive, but the omissions still matter. The form keeps the usual fields and procedure prompts in one place.

Current status

Personal prototype. Narrow on purpose. It covers a few common general surgery cases, not every operation in theatre.

Current prototype

Prompts for common emergency general surgery notes

  • Choose lap appendix, lap chole, diagnostic laparoscopy, abscess I&D or open inguinal hernia repair.
  • Fill in admin details, anaesthetic, indication, findings, specimen, drain, closure and post-op plan.
  • Use procedure-specific prompts and checks before copying the note.
  • The tool does not infer pathology, complications or clinical decisions.
  • It runs in the browser. There is no backend, login, cloud sync, analytics, external AI call or data upload.

Clinical safety boundary

Drafting aid only. Not a clinical system.

This is a personal tool for drafting text from information entered by a clinician. It is not an EHR, referral platform, NHS-approved product, clinical safety system, regulated medical device or source of truth.

Do not send patient-identifiable information to this website or repository. Check and correct the draft before entering it into the clinical record.

Operative Note Generator is an experimental personal project by Sangeev. Treat its output as a draft, not a finished operation note.
